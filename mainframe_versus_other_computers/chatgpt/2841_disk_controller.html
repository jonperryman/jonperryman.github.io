IBM 2841 Storage Control Unit

The IBM 2841 was a storage control unit introduced by IBM in the mid-1960s to work with the System/360 mainframe family.

It acted as the "middleman" between the CPU and the disk drives — translating system commands into actual read/write operations on the disks.

Key Facts about IBM 2841

Feature Details
:-------:--------
Announced 1965
Primary Role Interface between IBM System/360 and disk storage (2311, 2314 drives)
Connected to CPU via "selector channels" or "multiplexer channels"
Supported Devices IBM 2311 disk drives, IBM 2314 disk systems
Capacity Could manage multiple disk drives at once
Location Usually installed near the mainframe but physically separate

What Did the 2841 Actually Do?

  • Buffered communication between the CPU and the disks — allowing the CPU to move on while the 2841 handled the slower disk I/O operations.
  • Managed seeking (moving the disk arm), reading, and writing at the drive level.
  • Optimized data transfer across channels to prevent bottlenecks.
  • Handled Errors like retries if a disk read/write had a problem.
  • Multiplexed access to multiple drives — multiple operations could happen independently on different drives.
Without the 2841, the CPU would have to wait while every slow disk movement completed — meaning wasted time and poor performance.

Which Disk Systems Needed a 2841?

  • IBM 2311 Direct Access Storage Device (single drives)
  • IBM 2314 Direct Access Storage Facility (arrays of drives)

The 2314, for example, needed the 2841 to coordinate operations across all its drives.

Rough System Layout

Technical Note

  • The 2841 worked with selector channels (designed for fast, large data transfers) rather than just multiplexer channels (which juggled lots of small devices like card readers or printers).
  • Later systems improved on this design with more advanced controllers integrated into disk subsystems.

Fun Fact

  • To install a 2841 and a full 2314 disk complex, a company often had to remodel their data center — the floors had to support several tons of equipment, and the air conditioning had to be upgraded because of the heat produced!
