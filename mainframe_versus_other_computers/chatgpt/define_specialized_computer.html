A specialized computer is a computer system designed and optimized to perform a specific task or a limited range of tasks with high efficiency, accuracy, or speed. Unlike general-purpose computers, which can run a wide variety of applications, specialized computers are tailored for particular functions.

Key Characteristics of Specialized Computers:

  • Task-Specific: Built to do one or a few tasks extremely well (e.g., controlling a carâ€™s braking system or running medical imaging software).
  • Efficient: Highly optimized for performance, speed, or power consumption in their specific domain.
  • Limited Flexibility: Not ideal for running unrelated applications or adapting to new tasks without redesign.
