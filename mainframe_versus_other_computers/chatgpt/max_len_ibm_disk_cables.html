I asked ChatGPT: "max length of each type of disk drive cables?"

Awesome question — diving deep into classic mainframe tech! Let’s break it down clearly:

Each type of cable (used for disk drives and controllers) had a different maximum length based on how fast signals could travel without getting corrupted. Here's a rundown:

🖇️ Bus and Tag Cables (used in IBM mainframes like with the 3830/3330 systems)

  • Max Length:
    ➔ About 400 feet (≈120 meters)
  • Use: Connects CPU (mainframe) to I/O control units (like the 3830).
  • Type: Parallel copper cable (big and heavy).

📀 CKD Disk Cables (Count Key Data, for drives like IBM 3330, 3350)

  • Max Length:
    ➔ 100–200 feet (~30–60 meters) between disk drives and the control unit.
  • Use: Disk-specific cabling from control unit to the physical disk drives.
  • Type: Specialized shielded cable — shorter because it carried very sensitive timing data.

📼 ESCON (Enterprise Systems Connection) (Later generation — optical cable starting ~1990s)

  • Max Length:
    ➔ About 3 kilometers (1.9 miles) with optical repeaters.
    ➔ About 500 meters (1,600 feet) without repeaters.
  • Use: Replaced Bus and Tag; fiber optic channel for faster, longer connections.

🌐 FICON (Fibre Connection) (Modern mainframes — started late 1990s)

  • Max Length:
    ➔ 10 kilometers (6.2 miles) on multimode fiber
    ➔ Up to 100 kilometers (62 miles) on single-mode fiber with proper setups.
  • Use: High-speed fiber-optic links between mainframes and storage (SANS, DS8000, etc).
