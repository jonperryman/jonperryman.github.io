Alright, here’s a quick breakdown of maximum lengths for common disk cables:

  • SATA (Serial ATA) Data Cable
    ➔ Max length: 1 meter (about 3.3 feet)
        (Official spec recommends keeping it under this for stability.)
  • SATA Power Cable
    ➔ No strict official limit, but practically: around 18-24 inches (~45–60 cm) for typical PSU cables. (Longer ones exist with extensions.)
  • IDE/PATA Ribbon Cable (for old hard drives/CD drives)
    ➔ Max length: 18 inches (about 46 cm)
    (Any longer, and the data gets too corrupted.)
  • NVMe (M.2)
    ➔ Direct connection — no cable (it plugs into the motherboard).
  • U.2 (NVMe over cable)
    ➔ Max length: up to 1 meter for PCIe Gen3. (For Gen4 it needs to be shorter.)
  • USB for External Drives
    ➔ USB 2.0: up to 5 meters (~16 feet)
    ➔ USB 3.0/3.1: about 3 meters (~10 feet) max before needing powered hubs or signal boosters.
  • Thunderbolt (for high-speed external drives)
    ➔ Thunderbolt 3/4 cables:
    • Passive: 0.8 meters for full 40Gbps speed
    • Active: can go up to 2 meters at full speed, longer with some drop-off.
