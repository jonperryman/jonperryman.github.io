I asked Gemini "how many mainframes used by the irs"

The IRS has significantly consolidated its mainframe systems over the years.1 As of a consolidation effort that began in 1997, the number of mainframe computers used by the IRS was reduced from 67 to 20.2 These 20 mainframes are housed in only 3 locations, a reduction from the previous 20 locations.3


It's important to note that while the number of physical mainframes has decreased, the IRS still heavily relies on mainframe technology for its core operations, including processing tax returns and managing taxpayer data. They also run approximately 200 different software products in their mainframe environment. The IRS is actively working on modernizing these systems, including converting legacy code (like COBOL and Assembly Language Code) to more modern languages like Java.4
