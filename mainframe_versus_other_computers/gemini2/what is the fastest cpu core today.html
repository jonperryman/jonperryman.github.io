I asked Gemini "what is the fastest cpu core today"

As of mid-2025, the "fastest CPU core" can be interpreted in a few ways:

For overall gaming performance, the AMD Ryzen 7 9800X3D is generally considered the fastest chip currently available.1 Its 3D V-Cache technology significantly boosts gaming frame rates, outperforming even some of Intel's latest offerings in that specific workload.


For single-threaded performance, which is crucial for many applications and everyday responsiveness, the Intel Core Ultra 9 245K and AMD Ryzen 7 9700X are neck and neck, with the Intel chip having a slight edge in some benchmarks.

For a balance of gaming and productivity/multi-threaded performance, the AMD Ryzen 9 9950X3D is a top contender, while Intel's Core Ultra 9 285K also offers excellent performance in both areas.

It's important to note that the "fastest" CPU can depend heavily on the specific workload and applications you are using. Benchmarks for gaming often favor AMD's X3D series, while heavily multi-threaded professional applications might lean towards higher core count CPUs from either AMD or Intel.

Here are the general specifications for the AMD Ryzen 9 9950X3D and Intel Core Ultra 9 285K, as of mid-2025:

AMD Ryzen 9 9950X3D

  • Architecture: Zen 5 (Granite Ridge) with 2nd Gen 3D V-Cache Technology1

  • Cores/Threads: 16 Cores / 32 Threads2

  • Base Clock Frequency: 4.3 GHz3

  • Max Boost Clock Frequency: Up to 5.7 GHz4

  • L3 Cache: 128 MB (combines 32 MB standard L3 cache with 96 MB 3D V-Cache)

  • L2 Cache: 16 MB (1 MB per core)5

  • TDP (Thermal Design Power): 170W6

  • Socket: AM5 (LGA 1718)7

  • Memory Support: DDR5-5600 (officially supported, with overclocking potential)

  • PCI Express Version: PCIe 5.08

  • Integrated Graphics: AMD Radeon Graphics (2 Graphics Core Count, 2200 MHz frequency)9

  • Manufacturing Process: TSMC 4nm FinFET for CPU cores, 6nm FinFET for I/O Die10


Intel Core Ultra 9 285K

  • Architecture: Arrow Lake-S (Hybrid architecture with Performance-cores and Efficient-cores)

  • Cores/Threads: 24 Cores / 24 Threads (8 Performance-cores + 16 Efficient-cores)11

  • P-core Base Frequency: 3.7 GHz12

  • E-core Base Frequency: 3.2 GHz

  • Max Turbo Frequency: Up to 5.7 GHz (Intel Thermal Velocity Boost Frequency)13

  • L3 Cache: 36 MB Intel Smart Cache

  • Total L2 Cache: 40 MB

  • TDP (Thermal Design Power): 125W (Processor Base Power), 250W (Maximum Turbo Power)

  • Socket: LGA 1851

  • Memory Support: DDR5-6400 (officially supported)14

  • PCI Express Version: PCIe 5.0 and 4.015

  • Integrated Graphics: Intel Arc Xe-LPG Graphics (64EU)

  • NPU (Neural Processing Unit): Yes, with up to 13 TOPS performance

  • Manufacturing Process: TSMC 3nm

It's important to note that while both CPUs offer high performance, they achieve it through different architectural approaches. The AMD Ryzen 9 9950X3D leverages its large 3D V-Cache for exceptional gaming performance, while the Intel Core Ultra 9 285K utilizes a hybrid core design and an integrated NPU, indicating a focus on broader application performance and AI acceleration.16
