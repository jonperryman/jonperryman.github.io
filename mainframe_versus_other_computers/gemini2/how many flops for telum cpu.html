The IBM Telum processor and its successor, Telum II, are designed for enterprise-grade workloads, particularly in mainframes, and include integrated AI accelerators.1


For the original Telum processor, its on-chip AI accelerator provides over 6 teraflops (TFLOPs) of compute capacity per chip. When scaled up to a 32-chip system, this can reach over 200 TFLOPs.2


The newer Telum II processor features an integrated on-chip AI accelerator that offers 24 trillion operations per second (TOPS), which is a 4x improvement over the original Telum.3 When fully configured with eight Telum II processors in a drawer, it can achieve up to 192 TOPS of AI processing power.4 It's important to note that TOPS often refers to integer operations (INT8), while TFLOPS typically refers to floating-point operations.
